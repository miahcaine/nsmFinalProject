This visualization combines a map of NYC and a linked view of a timeline. The map will be colored in to represent the amount of stops were conducted in a given precinct. When a user clicks on a precinct, the timeline of stops for that given precinct will be shown to the right of the map, which is a linked view. Likely will included brushing for the timeline that will update the map with stops for the selected time period.
This visualization is part of the hook and will show how some communities were more heavily affected by stop and frisk policies by others.

This visualization will show victims by a particular demographic. The idea is to have an animation with 10,000 circles that represents total stop and frisk victims. When the user clicks to see more, the dots with transition to break down sex / age / race / build demographic to show the story regarding how it was often young Black men who were tge targets of stop and frisk. Currently I am using a packing layout which does not show the data like I want it to, so this week I plan on looking at the Syra-Visualized final project to see how I can do more pleasing transitions. After the initial transition, the user should be able to click on a particular demographic and have the circles filter / arrange based off of the selected demographic
