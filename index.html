Have Stop and Frisks Been Effective?

Over 2003-2016, stop and frisks were most often conducted for criminal possession of a weapon, grand larceny, robbery, burglary, and criminal trespass. However, whether the high frequency correlated with a corresponding decrease in respective crime rates is still being discussed today. Interact with the slider below to see how rates for these top five crimes have changed, if at all, over the time period.

Where are these stops occurring? And how frequently?

There were over 5 million stops between 2003 and 2016, peeking in 2011 as you can see by the timeline below. Some police precincts conducted more stops than others. Click on a precinct to show that precinct's timeline of stops, and hover over the timeline to see the number of stops per year.

Who was targeted?

Though the 'intention' of stop and frisk was to find criminals, stop and frisk seemed to target particular demographics while leaving other demographics relatively unaffected. Of the over 5 million stops between 2003 and 2016, let these 1000 dots be representative of all of the stops.
